Summary

In 1996 I ended my university studies and started working for ABB as a Lotus Notes administrator. I have since then worked with delivering IT services in more or less every possible aspect, having leading roles in different degrees.

It is my firm belief that the key to success in any service oriented business is people. With motivated employees and customers with the right expectations it is possible to take a service oriented business anywhere. The critical success factor to creating these prerequisites is the ability to build relations. I excel at building relations. I am truly a people person.

When it comes to solving problems my experiences have taught me that a pragmatic approach always is efficient. I am allergic to finger pointing and blame games since neither of them really helps finding a solution to any problem.

In the world of IT services everything is connected to everything in some way. In this environment it is important to be able to see the full picture and how things are related. This is one of my greatest strengths. I am holistic, systematic and analytical in the way I approach any task. I have found that these properties make good use in situations of change. It is also in situations of change I thrive and perform at my best.

As you can see on this website, web development is not my greatest strength. My skills are best used in agile leadership roles, such as scrum master or porduct owner. But I beleive that cheating a bit as a developer make me stronger in my core skillset.
